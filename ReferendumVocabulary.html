Abstract

REF, the Referendum Vocabulary,was created in order to describe referendum events and the connected data. It might be applied for discribing other voting events as well. The REF vocabulary allows to describe a referendum event with the theme of the voting, as well as the affected area by the voting. Further more it enables a description of the area, the voting takes place - a district - and the description of the voting data including how many people were allowed to vote, how many "Yes"-votes and "No"-votes were given. Invalid votes can be described as well.

3. Descriptions about REF

The REF definitions presented here are written using a computer language (RDF/OWL) for simplifying the processing of basic infromation about the terms of the Referendum Vocabulary and furthermore about the things beeing discribed by these terms using REF, by software.

3.1. Evolution and extension of REF

The Referendum Vocabulary is identified by the namespace URI "http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#".

Revisions and extensions of REF are conducted through edits to the namespace document, which by convention is published on the Web at the namespace URI.

The properties and types defined here provide some basic concepts for describing information relevant about voting events. Other vocabularies (e.g. AIISO - Academic Institution Internal Structure Ontology, BIBO - The Bibliographic Ontology, W3C Ontology for Media Resources, the Time Ontology in OWL, the Open Provenance Model Vocabulary, Semantic Publishing and Referencing Ontologies (SPAR), SWAN Ontology, GeoSPARQL etc.) can also be mixed in with REF terms. REF is designed to be extended.

4. Cross-reference for REF classes and properties

Classes:

Class: Referendum

Any kind of referendum event. This vocabulary might be used for e.g. describing a local referendum.
For example: The referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster can be classified with the class Referendum.

identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#Referendum

Class: District

Any kind of district, e.g. a city district.
For example: The voting of the referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster took place in different districts of the cities which can be classified by District.

identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#District

Properties:

Property: isAbout

Any kind of subject of a voting event.
For example: The referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster had the the subject of opening shops on additional Sundays in the Muenster city center.

Identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#isAbout
OWL Type:ObjectProperty
Domain: ref:Referendum
Range: xsd:string

[back to overview]


Property: affectedArea

Any area that will be affected by the result of a voting event.
For example: The referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster affected the shops in the city centre and parts of the street Hammerstr.

Identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#affectedArea
OWL Type:ObjectProperty
Domain: ref:Referendum
Range: geosparql:Geometry

[back to overview]


Property: hasVotingArea

Any districts which are allowed to take part in the voting event.
For example: The referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster had more voting districts than just the area which was affected by the referendum. This can be expressed by using hasVotingDistrict.

Identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#hasVotingDistrict
OWL Type:ObjectProperty
Domain: ref:Referendum
Range: ref:District

[back to overview]


Property: hasSubDistrict

Any subdevision of districts which are allowed to vote, in order to obtain a finer granuality of the voting results.
For example: The results from the referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster are available in a finer granuality than just the districts. This can be expressed by using hasSubDistrict.

Identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#hasSubDistrict
OWL Type:ObjectProperty
Domain: ref:District
Range: ref:District

[back to overview]


Property: hasTotalVoters

Any number of people who are alowed to vote during the particulat voting event.
For example: Not all people are allowed to vote at every voting event. During the referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster only citzien aged 18. and above of the city were allowed to vote.

Identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#hasTotalVoters
OWL Type:DatatypeProperty
Domain: ref:District
Range: xsd:integer

[back to overview]


Property: hasYesVoters

The number of people who voted with "YES" during a voting event.
For example: The referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster the voters were allowed to choose between "Yes" and "No", and the number of "Yes"-votes can be expressed by this property.

Identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#hasYesVotes
OWL Type:DatatypeProperty
Domain: ref:District
Range: xsd:integer

[back to overview]


Property: hasNoVotes

The number of people who voted with "NO" during a voting event.
For example: The referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster the voters were allowed to choose between "Yes" and "No", and the number of "No"-votes can be expressed by this property.

Identifier:http://course.geoinfo2016.org/G1/vocabulary/vocabulary/ref#hasNoVotes
OWL Type:DatatypeProperty

Property: hasInvalidVotes

The number of people made their voting invalid during the voting event.
For example: The referendum of November 6th 2016 in Muenster some people decided to make their vote invalid, so this can be expressed by hasInvalidVotes.
