Your Choice Counts!!

A framework for displaying results of the referendum of Münster.

Referendum Map Münster

Welcome to the Referendum Map Münster. Here you are provided with infomation regarding the recent referendum that took place in Münster on November 6th in 2016. The referendum took place to decide whether some shops should be open on two additional Sundays - for Hansetag and Herbstsent - or not. The affected shops are those of the city center and part of the Hammerstreet.

The polling stations were well distributed throughout Münster in order to get the opinion of as many people as possible in the form of votes. Here you can find a visual representation of the results from the different stations. The data presented is the number of people that were eligible for voting, the number of people who actually voted, invalid votes, valid votes, number of people in favor of this decision and the number of people against it.

The representation shows the city districts of Münster and the colours (green or red) of the corresponding areas denote the overall result from that particular station. For example the areas highlighted green show that the majority of voters of this area voted in support of the shops being opened on additional Sundays and areas colored red show the opposite outcome. Results are also displayed in the form of a pie chart in the pop-up.

Help

Your choice Counts!! helps you to visualize results of a referendum event.

In order to view the results of a particular voting district click on the district you are interested in. A pop-up displays a piechart showing the number of people who voted in favour of the referendums question and the number of people being against it.

  • Color green indicates that the maximum number of voters are in favour of the question of the referendum

  • Color red indicates that the maximum number of voters are against the question of the referendum

If available the pop-up also can contain additional information about the chosen area.

The layer selection in the top right corner allows to switch between different layers which differ in their level of detail of the voting result as well as the possibility to enable or disable the display of the affected area.

  • The layer Stadteile represents the division of the city with the lowest level of detail and can be translated into english with city districts

  • The layer Kommunalwahlbezirke is the devision of the city with a medium level of detail and can be translated into english with local election districts

  • The layer Stimmbezirke represents the division of the city with the highest level of detail and can be translated into english with constituency

    Your Choice Counts!

    The project was part of the masters course "Geoinformation in society" that took place at the Institute for Geoinformatics. The course "Geoinforamtion in Society" was about turning existing data to geographic linked open data and build a software application which consumes data and to visualize it.
