Referendum Map Münster

Welcome to the Referendum Map Münster. Here you will be provided with infomation regarding the recent referendum that took place in Münster on November 6th in 2017. This referendum took place so as to decide upon whether the shops on few Sundays before the Christmas should remain open or should be closed, as they are every Sunday.

The polling stations were well distributed throughout Münster in order to get the opinion of as many people in the shape of votes. Here is a visual representation of the results from all the different stations. The data that we are interested in is the number of people that were eligible for voting, the number of people that actually voted, invalid votes, valid votes, number of people in favor of this and number of people against this decision.

Our representation shows the major areas of Münster and the colours (Green or Red) of the corresponding areas denote the overall result from that particular station. For example the areas highlighted as green, show that majority of the voters in that area voted in support of the shops being opened on these Sundays and areas in red colour show the opposite trend. Apart from the this the results can also be seen in figures and on pie charts.
