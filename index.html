Referendum Map Münster

Welcome to the Referendum Map Münster. Here you are provided with infomation regarding the recent referendum that took place in Münster on November 6th in 2016. The referendum took place to decide whether some shops should be open on three additional Sundays or not. The affected shops are those in the two main shopping streets in the inner city (Salzstraße and Prinzipalmarkt).

The polling stations were well distributed throughout Münster in order to get the opinion of as many people in the form of votes. Here is a visual representation of the results from all the different stations. The data that we are interested in is the number of people that are eligible for voting, the number of people that actually voted, invalid votes, valid votes, number of people in favor of this and number of people against this decision.

Our representation shows the major areas of Münster and the colours (Green or Red) of the corresponding areas denote the overall result from that particular station. For example the areas highlighted as green, show that majority of the voters in that area voted in support of the shops being opened on these Sundays and areas in red colour show the opposite trend. Results are also displayed in the form of a pie chart on the pop-up.

Help

Your choice Counts!! helps you to visualize the results of the referendum of Muenster.

In order to view the results of a particular district click on the district which you are interested in. Then a pop up displays with a piechart showing the number of people that are favour towards opening the shops on sundays and number of people that are againest to it.

  • Color green indicates that the maximum number of people are in favour of opening the shops on sundays

  • Color Red indicates that the maximum number of people are againest to opening the shops on sundays

About

Your Choice Counts!

The project was part of the masters course "Geoinformation in society" that took place at the Institute for Geoinformatics. The course "Geoinforamtion in Society" was about turning existing data to geographic linked open data and build a software application which consumes data and to visualize it.
