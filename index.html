A framework for displaying results of the referendum of Münster.

Referendum Map Münster

Welcome to the Referendum Map Münster. Here you are provided with infomation regarding the recent referendum that took place in Münster on November 6th in 2016. The referendum took place to decide whether some shops should be open on two additional Sundays - for Hansetag and Herbstsent - or not. The affected shops are those of the city center and part of the Hammerstreet.

The polling stations were well distributed throughout Münster in order to get the opinion of as many people as possible in the form of votes. Here you can find a visual representation of the results from all the different stations. The data presented is the number of people that were eligible for voting, the number of people that actually voted, invalid votes, valid votes, number of people in favor of this decision and number of people against it.

The representation shows the major areas of Münster and the colours (Green or Red) of the corresponding areas denote the overall result from that particular station. For example the areas highlighted green show that majority of the voters of that area voted in support of the shops being opened on additional Sundays and areas in red colour show the opposite outcome. Results are also displayed in the form of a pie chart on the pop-up.

Help

Your choice Counts!! helps you to visualize the results of a referendum event.

In order to view the results of a particular district click on the district which you are interested in. Then a pop up displays with a piechart showing the number of people who voted in favour of the referendums question and the number of people being against it.

  • Color green indicates that the maximum number of voters are in favour of the question of the referendum

  • Color Red indicates that the maximum number of voters are against the question of the referendum

The layer selection in the top right corner allows to switch between different layers which differ in their levels of detail of the voting result as well as the possibility to enable the display of the affected area.

About

Your Choice Counts!

The project was part of the masters course "Geoinformation in society" that took place at the Institute for Geoinformatics. The course "Geoinforamtion in Society" was about turning existing data to geographic linked open data and build a software application which consumes data and to visualize it.
